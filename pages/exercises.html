Exercise: Release Numbness

Use when: you feel emotionally flat, disconnected, or unable to feel anything

About numbness: Numbness is the emotional body's pause button. It kicks in when feelings would be too much to process — grief, rage, shame. Sometimes we stop feeling at all. First you need to let yourself out of the numbness — then work with the actual feeling.

Use intention to let go of numbness and its side benefits: "I can't get hurt" and "I don't need to feel this."

1
Find a quiet space. Ideally do a meditation or breath work session first. Set the intention for numbness to release.
2
Build your light field. White sun in your heart → fills your body → expands to the size of the house, huge bright light around you.
3
Focus on your belly area and say out loud with very strong intention to free yourself: "Feeling of numbness doesn't serve me any more and I let it go."
4
Acknowledge the difference. Say: "I acknowledge the difference between being numb and not being hurt. I acknowledge the difference between being numb and being strong and having power."
5
Apply the 13 Transformations in the white light around your body as the numbness begins to soften.
6
Ground. Say out loud: "I put my roots deep into the earth and imagine how your roots get into the earth and you start receiving love form the planet." Imagine that you are a tree and you have roots.

Exercise: Pinpoint Your Trauma

Use when: you want to identify your specific emotional blocks and understand where they come from

All of us have emotional triggers — things that cause a disproportionately strong reaction. Often we don't know why. This exercise helps you trace the reaction back to its source. Understanding why something hurts is the beginning of healing it.

Exercise 1: Name It

1–2 times a day, pause and ask yourself: "What am I feeling right now?"

Take a breath and ask: "Why am I feeling this? What belief or experience is behind it?"

Write down what comes — even single words. Don't analyze, just notice.

Exercise 2: Body Map

Try to locate the feeling in your body: stomach, chest, throat, head? Notice texture — is it heavy, sharp, cold, hot?

Once you can locate it physically, you can work with it energetically. The body always knows more than the mind.

1
Identify a trigger. Think of a situation that regularly causes you distress — something that seems "too big" a reaction for what actually happened.
2
List the emotions. What exactly do you feel in that situation? Be specific: "I feel worthless and invisible," not just "bad." Name each emotion.
3
Ask: When did I first feel this? Let whatever comes, come — even if it seems unrelated or surprising. The emotional body is associative, not logical.
4
Understand the link. The external trigger exists because you carry an unhealed emotional wound at this frequency. The situation is resonating with what's already there — it didn't create it.
5
Apply the Full Healing Protocol to the root event you identified. See Healing Protocols.

Exercise: Find the Root of a Trauma

Use when: you know you have a pattern but can't find where it started

Once the trauma had started — past lives / ancestors. Go to the beginning of the emotions and instincts that activated your trauma. List the emotions and instincts. Find where it is in your body.

1
Enter relaxed awareness. White light field. Breathe slowly until you feel genuinely calmer and more present.
2
Go to the emotion — the specific feeling associated with the pattern. Feel it in your body without acting on it. Stay with it as an observer.
3
Ask: What is underneath this? Anger often covers grief. Grief often covers fear. Fear often covers worthlessness. Peel the layers by asking what's below each one.
4
Ask: Is this mine? Does this feeling belong to me, or did I absorb it from someone else — a parent, an ancestor, a collective experience? Sometimes the root isn't personal at all.
5
Apply 13 Transformations wherever you feel the root. Imagine the transformation signs in the white light directly in that area of your body or energy field.
6
Release and ground. As the root energy shifts, ground immediately — roots into the earth. Let the earth absorb what you released.

Emotions & Instincts Reference

Use this list when pinpointing a trauma. Start by identifying which emotions or instincts are active in the pattern you're working with.

  • Joy — the feeling of aliveness, expansion, delight
  • Sadness — natural response to loss; unprocessed it becomes grief
  • Fear — response to threat; when frozen becomes chronic anxiety
  • Anger — boundary violation signal; suppressed becomes resentment or rage
  • Disgust — rejection of what feels toxic or wrong
  • Surprise — response to the unexpected; can flip to shock
  • Love — connection, warmth, belonging; its absence feels like deprivation
  • Shame — "I am bad/wrong/defective" (vs guilt: "I did something bad")
  • Guilt — sense of having harmed or failed someone
  • Grief — deep sadness over sustained loss
  • Jealousy — fear of losing connection to someone you value
  • Envy — pain at seeing others have what you want
  • Resentment — anger that hasn't been expressed or resolved
  • Bitterness — resentment calcified over time
  • Contempt — sense of superiority masking insecurity
  • Loneliness — disconnection from others or from oneself
  • Helplessness — belief that nothing you do can change the outcome
  • Despair — hopelessness that a situation will ever improve
  • Fight — aggression, dominance, controlling behavior
  • Flight — avoidance, running from conflict or intimacy
  • Freeze — paralysis, numbness, shutdown
  • Fawn — people-pleasing, losing yourself to keep others calm
  • Submit — excessive compliance, difficulty saying no
  • Collapse — depression, inability to act, giving up
  • Gratitude — genuine appreciation that elevates frequency instantly
  • Compassion — warmth toward self and others without losing self
  • Acceptance — being with what is without needing it to be different
  • Forgiveness — releasing the charge of the past; doesn't mean condoning
  • Trust — confidence in life's intelligence and your own resilience
  • Curiosity — openness to what is, without judgment
  • Wonder — the feeling of being alive in an extraordinary world
  • Serenity — stillness that is not absence but fullness
Working with emotions tip: Practice recognising which emotions you're most disconnected from — those are usually the ones most worth working with. If you feel nothing when you try to access sadness, that's information. If anger never comes naturally, that too is data. The suppressed emotions are the ones running the most programs in the background.
